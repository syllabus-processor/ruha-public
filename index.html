What We're Building

Ruha is a small research and software effort focused on tools that support real clinical work—especially for solo practitioners, supervisors, and training environments.

Our primary system in development is EMA (Electronic Mental Health Application): a clinician-centered platform designed to support intake, documentation, and continuity of care while preserving clinical judgment and calm cognition.

EMA is not designed to optimize throughput or productivity. It is designed to stay out of the way of care.

What EMA Does Not Do

  • No analytics on clinical content
  • No productivity scoring or engagement metrics
  • No sale or secondary use of data
  • No model training on clinical material
  • No feature expansion without stability earned through real use

These constraints are architectural, not aspirational.

Governance & Oversight

EMA is developed under explicit design principles and clinician advisory oversight intended to prevent drift toward institutional or extractive software models. If a feature benefits procurement but harms clinicians or clients, it is not built.

Current Status

Observation Phase

EMA is currently in staged use and stability observation. New features are introduced only after demonstrating safe, predictable operation in real clinical workflows.
